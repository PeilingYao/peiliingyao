Public Economics

Content :

This lecture provides an advanced treatment of the central topics in Public Economics.

We first study the classic problems of optimal (income and commodity) taxation. Second, we consider the potential role of government arising from market failures. Throughout these analyses, we will be adopting a traditional Public Finance perspective, which treats the government as a unitary benevolent actor. In the final part of the course, we discuss Public Choice Theory as a modern alternative, and consider economic theories of group decision making, voting, and multilateral bargaining.

Literature :

Hindriks, J. & G. Myles (2006). Intermediate Public Economics, MIT [HM]

Mueller, D. (2003) Public Choice III, Cambridge University [MU]

Prof.Dr.Christoph Vanberg
